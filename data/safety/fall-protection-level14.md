# SAFE-FP-001: Fall Protection — Level 14

**Document ID:** SAFE-FP-001  
**Applies to:** Riverside Tower — Level 14 and above  
**Owner:** Site Safety / EHS  
**Effective date:** 2025-01-15

## Purpose

Ensure all personnel working at heights above 6 feet on Level 14 are protected from fall hazards.

## Minimum requirements (elevated work)

| Condition | Required protection |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Work above 6 ft at leading edge | Full-body harness + approved anchor |
| Perimeter without guardrails | Harness tied off to rated anchor point |
| Open floor penetrations | Covers rated for 2× intended load or guarded |

## Anchor points

- Use only anchors tagged **AP-L14-*** on the Level 14 layout drawing
- Never tie off to guardrail posts, conduit, or rebar projections
- One anchor per worker unless using a certified Y-lanyard

## Procedure before elevated work

1. Confirm fall protection plan is posted for the work zone.
2. Inspect harness and lanyard for wear, fraying, or damaged hardware.
3. Verify anchor point rating (minimum 5,000 lb per worker).
4. Brief crew on rescue plan before work begins.
5. Document inspection in the daily safety log.

## Generator pad / mezzanine work

For work on the Level 14 generator pad or mezzanine:
- Guardrails required on all open sides, or 100% tie-off
- Material staging must not block egress paths
- Tools must be tethered when working above occupied areas

## Supervisor responsibilities

- Stop work if fall protection is missing or improperly rigged
- Report repeat violations to EHS within the shift
- Ensure subcontractor crews comply before granting access to Level 14

## Emergency exception

There is **no exception** to fall protection requirements based on schedule urgency.
