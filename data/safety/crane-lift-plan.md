# SAFE-CRN-003: Crane Lift Plan & Exclusion Zone

**Document ID:** SAFE-CRN-003  
**Applies to:** Riverside Tower — all crane lifts  
**Owner:** Site Safety / Crane Operations  
**Effective date:** 2025-01-15

## Purpose

Ensure all crane lifts are planned, approved, and executed within a controlled exclusion zone.

## When a lift plan is required

- Any pick over 5,000 lb
- Any lift over occupied areas or active work zones
- Tandem lifts (two cranes)
- Lifts within 20 ft of energized power lines

## Lift plan approval process

1. Crane operator submits lift plan form **LF-CRN-001** 24 hours before the pick.
2. Rigging supervisor reviews rigging configuration and load weight.
3. Site superintendent approves the plan and signs off.
4. Exclusion zone barricades installed before the lift begins.

## Exclusion zone requirements

- Barricade full swing radius plus 10 ft buffer
- Only essential personnel inside the zone during the pick
- Spotter and signal person assigned and briefed
- No personnel under suspended loads at any time

## Wind limits

| Crane type | Maximum wind speed |
|------------|-------------------|
| Tower crane | 25 mph (sustained) |
| Mobile crane | 20 mph (sustained) |

Stop all lifting if wind exceeds limits or gusts create load swing.

## Stop-work authority

Any worker may call an **all-stop** if:
- Rigging appears damaged or improperly attached
- Load swing approaches personnel or structures
- Communication with signal person is lost

## After lift completion

- Remove barricades only after load is landed and secured
- Document lift in the daily crane log
- Report any near-miss to site safety within the shift

## Emergency exception

There is **no schedule exception** to lift plan requirements. Weather or wind delays are mandatory holds.
