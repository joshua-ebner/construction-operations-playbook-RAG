# SAFE-EXC-002: Excavation & Trenching Clearance

**Document ID:** SAFE-EXC-002  
**Applies to:** Riverside Tower — all excavation zones  
**Owner:** Site Safety / EHS  
**Effective date:** 2025-01-15

## Purpose

Ensure excavations and trenches are safely prepared, shored, and cleared before work begins.

## Before breaking ground

1. Call **811** for utility locate — wait 48 hours before excavation.
2. Confirm locate marks are visible and match the site plan.
3. Assign a **competent person** for the excavation (per OSHA 1926 Subpart P).
4. Brief crew on spoil pile placement (minimum 2 ft from trench edge).

## Shoring requirements

| Trench depth | Required action |
|--------------|-----------------|
| 0–4 ft | Sloping or benching per soil classification |
| 4–5 ft | Protective system required (shoring, shield, or sloping) |
| > 5 ft | Protective system mandatory; competent person on site |

## Utility clearance rules

- Hand-dig within **24 inches** of marked utilities
- Do not use mechanical excavation within 24 in of gas or electric lines
- If unmarked utility is exposed, stop work and notify superintendent immediately

## Entry and access

- Ladders required for trench access every 25 ft of trench length
- No personnel in trench while equipment operates within swing radius
- Atmospheric testing required if trench depth exceeds 4 ft in clay or near sewers

## Stop-work triggers

Stop excavation and evacuate the trench if:
- Water infiltration exceeds pumping capacity
- Visible cracking or sloughing of trench walls
- Vibration from adjacent operations causes soil movement

## Supervisor sign-off

Superintendent must sign the daily excavation log before crews enter a trench deeper than 4 ft.
