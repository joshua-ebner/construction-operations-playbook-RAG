# MAINT-PMP-204: Concrete Pump — Troubleshooting Guide

**Document ID:** MAINT-PMP-204  
**Equipment:** Concrete pump CP-RT-02, Riverside Tower  
**Owner:** Field Operations / Equipment  
**Effective date:** 2025-01-15

## Normal operating pressure

Normal line pressure during pour: **800–1,200 PSI** at steady state.

## Symptom: low pressure during pour

1. Check slump — mix below 3 in may cause blockage (notify QC, do not add water).
2. Read pressure gauge on panel **PG-PMP-02**.
3. Inspect boom tip for hardened concrete buildup.
4. Attempt short reverse pulse (3 seconds max) per section 4.2 if pressure will not recover.

## Symptom: line blockage

- Stop pour immediately
- Do not increase pressure above **1,500 PSI**
- Isolate the affected section of line
- Contact pump operator supervisor before disassembly

## When to stop and secure equipment

Stop the pour and secure the pump if:
- Pressure falls below **600 PSI** and does not recover in 2 cycles
- Visible hose rupture or concrete spray
- Unusual vibration from the pump truck chassis
- Hydraulic fluid leak at manifold **HYD-PMP-M**

## Bleed procedure (after shutdown)

1. Engage pump neutral and shut off engine.
2. Relieve line pressure using bleed valve **BV-PMP-01**.
3. Confirm gauge reads 0 PSI before disconnecting any coupling.
4. Chock pump truck wheels before maintenance access.

## Escalation

Contact pump vendor service if unresolved within 30 minutes. Reference asset tag **CP-RT-02**.

## Coordination with QC

If pour is stopped due to equipment failure during an active hold point, notify QC lead before resuming. See **QC-CON-004** for hold point rules.
