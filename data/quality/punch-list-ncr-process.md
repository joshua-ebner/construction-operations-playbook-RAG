# QC-NCR-100: Punch List & Nonconformance Process

**Document ID:** QC-NCR-100  
**Applies to:** Riverside Tower — all trades  
**Owner:** Quality Control  
**Effective date:** 2025-01-15

## When to open an NCR

- Concrete or rebar fails hold point inspection
- Repeated defect pattern (3 occurrences in 7 days)
- Work installed without required QC sign-off
- Client or architect rejection of installed work

## Process

1. Tag and segregate affected work area (red hold tag)
2. Open NCR in project QMS within 4 hours
3. Notify superintendent and responsible trade foreman
4. Disposition: rework, accept with deviation, or demolish and replace (requires QC manager approval)

## Interaction with field operations

If root cause is equipment-related, attach equipment work order to the NCR. Do not resume work in the affected area until corrective action is verified.

## Punch list coordination

- Punch list items linked to NCRs must be closed before area turnover
- Superintendent reviews open NCR count weekly in OAC meeting
- Zero-defect goal: no critical NCRs open at substantial completion

## Resume conditions

Work may resume in the affected area only after:
- Corrective action completed and inspected
- QC sign-off documented in QMS
- Superintendent notified of release

## Escalation

NCRs open longer than 5 business days escalate to project manager and QC manager.
