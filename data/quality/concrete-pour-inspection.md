# QC-CON-004: Concrete Pour Inspection — Level 14

**Document ID:** QC-CON-004  
**Applies to:** Riverside Tower — Level 14 concrete pours  
**Owner:** Quality Control  
**Effective date:** 2025-01-15

## Pre-pour checklist

1. Verify rebar placement per **QC-RBR-012** tolerances
2. Confirm formwork clean, oiled, and braced
3. Check embeds and sleeves per approved shop drawings
4. Verify concrete mix design ticket matches submittal
5. QC inspector sign-off required before first truck arrives

## Acceptance criteria (at placement)

| Property | Target | Acceptable range |
|----------|--------|------------------|
| Slump | 5 in | 4–6 in |
| Concrete temperature | 65–75 °F | 50–85 °F |
| Air content (exterior) | 6% | 5.5–7.5% |

Out-of-range loads require superintendent notification and QC hold.

## Hold point rules

Once a pour hold point is declared:
- No structural steel erection above or adjacent to the pour zone until cure hold is released
- No heavy equipment traffic within 15 ft of fresh concrete
- Curing blankets must remain in place for minimum **72 hours** (or per spec section 03 30 00)
- QC must verify compressive strength test cylinder break before release

## Active cure hold

When a cure hold is active on Level 14:
- Steel erection on Level 14 must wait until QC releases the hold
- Crane picks over the pour zone are prohibited
- The only exception is an **immediate life-safety emergency** (evacuation, rescue)

## Documentation

Record pour data in field log **FL-CON-L14**. Retain cylinder break results on file before hold release.

## Release authority

Only a **QC inspector or superintendent** may release a pour hold after criteria are met.
